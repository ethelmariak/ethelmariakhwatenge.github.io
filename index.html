Design Process

I use a blended ADDIE and backward design approach, starting from the intended learning outcomes and working backwards to align assessments, activities, and content.

Click each step to explore what I do in that phase.

1 Analysis: understand the learning problem ▾

Goals

  • Clarify the business or educational problem.
  • Understand the target learners, constraints, and context.
  • Agree on success metrics with stakeholders.

What I do

  • Review existing materials, data, and prior courses.
  • Hold short interviews or surveys with stakeholders and sample learners.
  • Summarise findings in a concise needs analysis document.

Outputs

  • Needs analysis summary.
  • Draft list of learning objectives and constraints.
2 Design: plan outcomes, assessments, and flow ▾

Goals

  • Write clear, measurable learning outcomes.
  • Design assessments and practice activities that align with outcomes.
  • Structure content into a logical, learner‑friendly journey.

What I do

  • Use backward design to map outcomes → assessments → activities.
  • Create course maps, lesson outlines, and storyboards/scripts.
  • Select interaction types (e.g., scenarios, knowledge checks, reflection prompts).

Outputs

  • Course map / design blueprint.
  • Storyboards or scripts for key screens and media.
3 Development: build and prototype ▾

Goals

  • Translate the design into functional learning experiences.
  • Ensure accessibility and responsive behaviour across devices.

What I do

  • Develop modules in tools like Articulate Rise 360, Storyline, or H5P.
  • Create supporting resources (PDF guides, job aids, checklists).
  • Build and share early prototypes for quick feedback.

Outputs

  • Working e‑learning modules or prototypes.
  • Downloadable resources for learners and facilitators.
4 Implementation: launch and support ▾

Goals

  • Deploy the course smoothly in the LMS.
  • Support facilitators and learners with clear guidance.

What I do

  • Configure the course in Canvas / Reach360 (enrolment, grading, pacing).
  • Set up announcements, orientation content, and help resources.
  • Brief facilitators on key activities and assessment expectations.

Outputs

  • Live course instance with open or controlled enrolment.
  • Facilitator guide or quick‑start instructions.
5 Evaluation: measure and iterate ▾

Goals

  • Gather evidence of learning and course effectiveness.
  • Identify specific improvements for the next iteration.

What I do

  • Analyse quiz results, completion data, and learner feedback.
  • Run short debriefs with stakeholders and facilitators.
  • Document changes for version 2+ of the course.

Outputs

  • Evaluation summary and recommendations.
  • Prioritised improvement list for future updates.
