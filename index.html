I use a blended ADDIE and backward design approach. I start by clarifying the desired learning outcomes and success criteria, then work backwards to design assessments and learning activities that align with those outcomes. Understand the performance gap and learning problem. Identify learner characteristics, constraints, and context. Document stakeholder expectations and success metrics. Review existing materials, data, and prior courses. Conduct short interviews or surveys with stakeholders and sample learners. Create a concise needs analysis summary. Needs analysis summary Prioritized learning objectives (draft) Translate needs into measurable learning objectives. Plan assessments and practice activities that align with objectives. Structure content into a coherent learning journey. Write SMART learning objectives and map them to assessments. Create a high‑level content outline and lesson flow. Develop storyboards or scripts for key interactions and media. Design blueprint / course map Storyboards or scripts Build learning materials that match the design blueprint. Ensure accessibility and responsiveness across devices. Author e‑learning modules in tools such as Articulate Rise 360 or Storyline. Develop supporting resources (job aids, PDFs, templates). Implement basic analytics or tracking in the LMS. Published e‑learning modules Downloadable resources (PDFs, worksheets, checklists) Deploy the course and support instructors or facilitators. Prepare learners with clear instructions and expectations. Upload and configure the course in the LMS (e.g., Canvas, Reach360). Set up enrollment options, announcements, and guidance for learners. Brief facilitators on key activities and assessments. Live course site Facilitator or instructor guide Gather evidence of learning and course effectiveness. Identify opportunities to refine content and delivery. Review quiz results, completion data, and learner feedback. Conduct short debriefs with stakeholders and facilitators. Prioritize and document revisions for the next iteration.
